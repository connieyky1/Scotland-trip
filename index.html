What to See

Top Attractions

🏰 Edinburgh Castle
~£22
The iconic fortress perched on volcanic rock — the heart of Edinburgh. See the Scottish Crown Jewels, Stone of Destiny, and the Great Hall. Watch the One O'Clock Gun fired from the north walls daily.
Arrive at 9:30am opening to beat the crowds. Book tickets online in advance — they sell out.
🦆 Arthur's Seat
Free
Edinburgh's ancient extinct volcano rising 251m above the city. A 30–45 minute hike from Holyrood Park to 360° panoramic views — the Firth of Forth, castle, and on clear days, the Highlands.
Wear proper shoes — can be slippery in April. Go on a clear morning for the best views.
🏛️ Palace of Holyroodhouse
~£18
The King's official residence in Scotland, at the foot of the Royal Mile. Rich history — Mary Queen of Scots lived here. Beautiful state apartments and ruins of Holyrood Abbey.
Closed Thursdays & Wednesdays outside peak season. Sunday is ideal. Book in advance.
⛪ Greyfriars Kirk & Bobby
Free
16th-century church with a famous kirkyard that inspired JK Rowling — spot the Tom Riddle gravestone. The loyal Skye Terrier Greyfriars Bobby waited at his master's grave for 14 years.
Don't rub Greyfriars Bobby's nose — locals consider it bad luck and it has damaged the statue.
🎨 Scottish National Gallery
Free
World-class art collection in a stunning neoclassical building. Titian, Raphael, Rembrandt and a superb collection of Scottish paintings. One of the UK's finest galleries — totally free.
Check for temporary exhibitions — usually also free or very cheap.
⛰️ Calton Hill
Free
Easy climb for spectacular city panoramas including the castle, Arthur's Seat, and Firth of Forth. Home to Nelson's Monument and the unfinished National Monument. Beautiful at sunrise and sunset.
Perfect on Day 1 arrival evening — watch the sunset over the city.
🌊 Victoria Street & Grassmarket
Free
Victoria Street's colourful curved cobbled lane (thought to have inspired Diagon Alley) leads down to Grassmarket Square — once the site of public executions, now buzzing with pubs, cafés and markets.
Great Saturday morning market in Grassmarket — check locally for April market dates.
🌸 Royal Botanic Garden
Free
70 acres of beautiful gardens — spectacular in April with spring blooms and cherry blossoms. The glasshouses are particularly impressive. One of the UK's finest botanic gardens.
Garden entry is free; glasshouses have a small charge. Perfect for a peaceful afternoon.
🏘️ Dean Village & Circus Lane
Free
Dean Village is a picturesque 19th-century mill village along the Water of Leith — feels like Beauty and the Beast. Nearby Circus Lane is Edinburgh's most Instagrammable cobbled street.
15-minute walk from Princes Street. Go in the morning for soft light and fewer people.

Be Prepared

Tips & Packing

🌦️

Edinburgh in April

Expect 8–14°C. Cool, sometimes windy with a chance of showers any day. But also beautiful bright spring days — the light is magical. Layers are your best friend.

🗺️ Before You Go
📅
Book attractions in advance. Edinburgh Castle, Holyroodhouse and popular restaurants fill up. Book online before you travel.
🍽️
Make dinner reservations now. Noto, Timberyard and Devil's Advocate can be hard to get. Book 1–2 weeks ahead.
💷
Scottish banknotes are different. Scotland uses British pounds (£) but notes look different to English ones. Both are valid — don't be surprised!
🚶 Getting Around
👟
Walk everywhere in Old Town. The city centre is very compact and walkable. Cobblestones are everywhere — wear comfortable shoes.
🚌
Lothian Buses for New Town & Stockbridge. Reliable and cheap — you can pay contactless on every bus. No exact change needed.
🚕
Taxis are metered and reasonable. City Cabs Edinburgh is reliable. Uber also operates here.
🏴󠁧󠁢󠁳󠁣󠁴󠁿 Culture & Etiquette
🥃
Order "whisky" not "Scotch". In Scotland, you ask for whisky. Scotch is what the rest of the world calls it — locals will smile knowingly.
🐕
Don't rub Greyfriars Bobby's nose. Tourists rub it for luck — but locals hate it and it has damaged the statue. Just admire!
🌧️
Don't be put off by the weather. Edinburgh rain is usually light and brief. Keep a small umbrella or waterproof jacket and carry on exploring.
💰 Money Saving
🆓
So much is free! Scottish National Gallery, Royal Botanic Garden, Calton Hill, Arthur's Seat, Dean Village, Holyrood Park — all free.
🍽️
Set lunch menus save money. Many fine restaurants offer great set lunch deals for a fraction of dinner prices — The Outsider is a perfect example.
